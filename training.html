Neo Cold Calling Training Center

A simple playbook for helping small businesses stop missing calls with Neo at Your Service.

You are not selling AI. You are helping small businesses answer calls they are already missing.

Start Here

Your job is not to sell complicated AI technology. Your job is to help the owner see a simple problem: when they're busy, on a job, driving, closed, or helping another customer, incoming calls may go unanswered. Neo helps catch those calls.

Key positioning

  • Neo is a bilingual AI receptionist — answers 24/7.
  • It captures the caller's name, phone number, and what they need, then sends the owner the details.
  • Great for overflow, after-hours, spam filtering, and Spanish-speaking callers.
  • Frees up whoever answers the phones — often a spouse or office person — for higher-value work like marketing and follow-ups.
  • Learns the business from their website — Neo builds a knowledge file from their site so it answers customers' actual questions, not just takes a message. No website? We build them a simple one at no additional charge.
  • Not just a missed-call safety net — Neo works every call live: it greets, qualifies, books the appointment, and can put the caller through to the owner. It's their front line on the phone, not only a backstop for the calls they miss.
  • Keep it honest: Neo is not a human employee and doesn't do everything a person does — but don't undersell it either. The line to land: "every call answered and every lead worked — so you stop losing business to a ringing phone," never "you never need staff again."

Goal of the Call

The goal is one thing: get them on the demo line right now to hear Neo answer for their business — nothing more.

The one goal

Ask if they have a minute to call the demo line right now — they hear Neo answer for their own business, then role-play as if they're one of their own customers.

Nothing more. Not "I'll send a link to try later," not a scheduled follow-up call, not asking to speak with the owner separately. The whole win is them hearing Neo, live, on their business.

Rules

  • Don't pressure. Don't argue. Don't overexplain.
  • Ask simple questions. Listen to the answer.
  • Ask for one clear next step.

Daily Call Targets

Daily target

  • 50–75 dials
  • 8–12 conversations
  • 3–5 owner conversations
  • 2–4 live demos placed (they call the demo line while you're on with them)

The most important number is not total dials — it's owner conversations.

Discovery Questions

Use only 2 or 3 per call. Ask, then listen.

  • When you're on a job or tied up, who answers your phone?
  • When you miss a call, does it usually go to voicemail?
  • Do you ever lose jobs because you couldn't answer fast enough?
  • Do you get many Spanish-speaking callers?
  • Do calls after 5 PM or on weekends go to voicemail?
  • Do you get a lot of spam or price-shoppers?
  • What is one new customer worth to you?

Role Play prompt
You are a small business owner receiving a cold call from a salesperson offering an AI receptionist called Neo at Your Service. Act realistic. Be skeptical but not rude at first. Ask common questions like "How much is it?", "Is this a robot?", "I already have voicemail," "I'm busy," and "Send me info." The salesperson's goal is not to pressure you. Their goal is to ask what happens to missed calls, explain Neo clearly, and get you to call the demo line right now to hear Neo answer for your business — then play customer and test it. That live demo is the whole goal; a texted number is only a fallback if you truly can't call right now. After the roleplay, give the salesperson feedback on: 1. Whether the opener sounded natural. 2. Whether they asked good discovery questions. 3. Whether they explained the product simply. 4. Whether they handled objections well. 5. Whether they asked for a clear next step.

Do / Don't Rules

Do

  • Speak slowly. Smile while talking.
  • Ask simple questions. Listen more than you talk.
  • Say "AI receptionist," not "robot."
  • Focus on missed calls.
  • Get them to call the demo line — live, right now.

Don't

  • Don't argue or talk too much.
  • Don't promise perfect performance.
  • Don't say Neo is human.
  • Don't claim it fully replaces staff.
  • Don't say Neo books your calendar by itself — appointments flow through the CRM/scheduling connection.
  • Don't pressure the owner.

Compliance / Number Protection

  • Use a live human caller for cold outreach.
  • Do not use AI voice or prerecorded calls for cold outreach.
  • Respect "do not call me" requests immediately.
  • Keep an internal do-not-call list.
  • No voicemail drops.
  • Do not mass-text cold prospects.
  • Do not spoof caller ID.
  • Do not cold call from 602-469-1111.
  • Use only approved outbound prospecting numbers.
