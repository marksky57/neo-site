Neo Cold Calling Training Center

A simple playbook for helping small businesses stop missing calls with Neo at Your Service.

You are not selling AI. You are helping small businesses answer calls they are already missing.

Start Here

Your job is not to sell complicated AI technology. Your job is to help the owner see a simple problem: when they're busy, on a job, driving, closed, or helping another customer, incoming calls may go unanswered. Neo helps catch those calls.

Key positioning

  • Neo is a bilingual AI receptionist — answers 24/7.
  • It captures the caller's name, phone number, and what they need, then sends the owner the details.
  • Great for overflow, after-hours, spam filtering, and Spanish-speaking callers.
  • It is not a human employee and not a magic replacement for staff.
  • It's a simple missed-call safety net.

Goal of the Call

The goal is to get one clear next step — nothing more.

Preferred outcomes

  1. Permission to send the demo link.
  2. Permission to speak with the owner.
  3. A short follow-up call.
  4. Interest in trying Neo.

Rules

  • Don't pressure. Don't argue. Don't overexplain.
  • Ask simple questions. Listen to the answer.
  • Ask for one clear next step.

Daily Call Targets

Daily target

  • 50–75 dials
  • 8–12 conversations
  • 3–5 owner conversations
  • 2–4 demo links sent
  • 1–2 follow-ups scheduled

The most important number is not total dials — it's owner conversations.

Gatekeeper Script

Getting to the owner
Hi, this is Mark Antonowsky with Clearline Answering Services. I'm trying to reach the owner or whoever handles incoming calls for the business. We help small businesses answer missed calls with a bilingual AI receptionist. Who would be the best person to speak with about that?

If they ask "What is this about?"

"It's about helping the business answer calls when everyone is busy, after hours, or when Spanish-speaking callers come in."

If the owner is unavailable

"No problem. What's the best way to send them a quick demo?"

Discovery Questions

Use only 2 or 3 per call. Ask, then listen.

  • When you're on a job or tied up, who answers your phone?
  • When you miss a call, does it usually go to voicemail?
  • Do you ever lose jobs because you couldn't answer fast enough?
  • Do you get many Spanish-speaking callers?
  • Do calls after 5 PM or on weekends go to voicemail?
  • Do you get a lot of spam or price-shoppers?
  • What is one new customer worth to you?

Roleplay Practice

Paste this into ChatGPT (or any AI) and practice until it feels natural.

Roleplay prompt
You are a small business owner receiving a cold call from a salesperson offering an AI receptionist called Neo at Your Service. Act realistic. Be skeptical but not rude at first. Ask common questions like "How much is it?", "Is this a robot?", "I already have voicemail," "I'm busy," and "Send me info." The salesperson's goal is not to pressure you. Their goal is to ask what happens to missed calls, explain Neo clearly, and get permission to send a demo link or schedule a short follow-up. After the roleplay, give the salesperson feedback on: 1. Whether the opener sounded natural. 2. Whether they asked good discovery questions. 3. Whether they explained the product simply. 4. Whether they handled objections well. 5. Whether they asked for a clear next step.

Do / Don't Rules

Do

  • Speak slowly. Smile while talking.
  • Ask simple questions. Listen more than you talk.
  • Say "AI receptionist," not "robot."
  • Focus on missed calls.
  • Ask permission to send the demo.

Don't

  • Don't argue or talk too much.
  • Don't promise perfect performance.
  • Don't say Neo is human.
  • Don't claim it fully replaces staff.
  • Don't overpromise appointment booking.
  • Don't pressure the owner.

Compliance / Number Protection

  • Use a live human caller for cold outreach.
  • Do not use AI voice or prerecorded calls for cold outreach.
  • Respect "do not call me" requests immediately.
  • Keep an internal do-not-call list.
  • No voicemail drops.
  • Do not mass-text cold prospects.
  • Do not spoof caller ID.
  • Do not cold call from 602-469-1111.
  • Use only approved outbound prospecting numbers.
